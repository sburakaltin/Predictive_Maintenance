#### A R T I C L E I N F O

A B S T R A C T

Keywords: Photovoltaic Predictive maintenance Anomaly detection Fault diagnosis Machine learning

Efforts to reduce the increase in average global warming by utilizing renewable energy continue to increase. One of the efforts is to build a solar power plant that is able to generate electricity by converting solar energy into electricity through a photovoltaic (PV) system. The existence of abundant, free, and year-round solar energy makes solar power plant promising in replacing fossil fuels used in thermal power plant. This paper aims to propose a predictive maintenance approach for PV systems using anomaly detection and fault diagnosis. In this study, the daily patterns of irradiance and corresponding AC output from a newly completed solar PV farm are investigated. Given the unlabelled nature of the data, traditional supervised learning methods are unsuitable for anomaly detection in this context. To address this, a long short-term memory autoencoder (LSTM-AE) model is employed to detect anomalies in the time series data. The LSTM-AE model is trained to reconstruct normal operation patterns, and deviations from these reconstructions are flagged as potential anomalies. This approach enables us to identify irregularities in the plant’s performance that could indicate system faults or inefficiencies, ultimately providing valuable insights into the maintenance and optimization of solar PV operations. The results show that the anomaly prediction can achieve reasonable accuracy with minimum test errors MSE, RMSE, and MAE of 10.95, 3.30, and 2.76, respectively, and can be applied to the PV system.

### 1. Introduction

The trend in using sustainable and renewable energy is increasing as well as raising energy demand both in industrial or housing sectors [1]. The emerging global issue of green energy is also pushing the government of many countries to create the policy that fosters the using of renewable energy. Solar energy is one of potential renewable energy resources that abundant and free to be utilized in year along. Solar energy can be used directly as in solar hot water collectors [2] or used to generate electricity with photovoltaic technology. Even hybrid technology is also being developed by combining photovoltaic technology to convert photon energy into electricity while the waste heat is used as a thermal water collector. This technology is known as the solar photovoltaic thermal water collector system [3]. The investment needed to solar energy harvesting is installation of PV system that convert the photon energy in the solar become electricity and store this in battery

storage system. In large capacity this system is called solar power plant system. However, with the growth of electricity production by PV system, some challenges also arise, for example, things related to performance of efficiency and suitable functioning of energy system. The such challenges must be considered in solar PV industry pertains to the program of replacing fossil fuels in thermal power plant and integration with other renewable electricity power resources such as wind and hydro power plant.

Along with the increasing use of PV system, the concern of maintenance for PV system also gains attention from researchers and expert. Although the quality of PV technology is now showing less demanding of maintenance since the manufacturer can guarantee of 20 years or even more operating time. However, the defects in PV system could be from climate or weather conditions [4–5], inherent substances in the air that trigger surface defects [6] and deficiency problem during manufacturing process that can escalate the failure’s probability in PV system [7]. In PV system, modules are constructed and connected to form a series-parallel topology where several cells are connected in series to form a string to gain optimal voltage. If the strings are mismatch, it will decrease the overall system performances. The such string is then connected to another string in parallel to form array of PV system. Furthermore, PV array is linked to the inverter which converts electricity from DC to AC. Selection of proper inverter is also important to avoid low conversion efficiency. To reach maximum electricity generation, PV panels and other component must work together in its best performance. If anomalous or faults go undetected, this will lead to loss maximum electricity generation.

Therefore, in response to the issue of faults or degradation in PV system, it is suggested to select and utilize suitable maintenance strategy which is capable for monitoring and detecting faults at early stage. Predictive maintenance (PdM) strategy is one of solutions that can perform condition monitoring, fault diagnosis dan prognosis of PV system. Usually, PdM requires many sensors to fulfill functionality in condition monitoring and provide information of actual conditions. Although PdM seems powerful as appropriate method for solar plant maintenance, however there is still gaps in the implementations, i.e., the cost for collecting the desired input data PV is expensive [11]. In addition, the architecture of PdM applied to solar power generation is complicated and requires a large amount of data for training and testing process. The variability of data input from sensor also high towards electricity output which tends to be fluctuated [12]. In case of generalization, PdM models usually tends be location-specific, this means it could not be applied for PV system with different location due to the difference of characteristics of data input when PdM models is built [13].

PdM models for PV system is generally developed by utilizing solar irradiance and ambient temperature data as training data and predict the active power as target data under normal operating conditions. Since the normal operating conditions are basis data used to develop the models, anomaly detection can be carried out by evaluating deviation of actual data from the baseline normal conditions. When an anomaly is detected, fault diagnosis can be conducted by applying diagnosis rules for fault justification at the system. In addition, PdM models is beneficial and can be constructed to optimize the scheduling of maintenance on a cost-effective basis before component of the system bereave their integrity during operation [14].

This paper deals with the development of predictive maintenance for PV System that equipped by features for anomaly detection, diagnosis and prognosis. Data used to develop the proposed method is real data

acquired from PV System by using SCADA system. Sensor parameters involved in system development is ambient temperature, humidity, solar irradiance, wind direction and wind speed while the formulated parameter used is active power AC. Historical data of such parameters are utilized to train LSTM-AE for generating model prediction. The trained LSTM-AE is then used to estimate the active power AC produced by inverter. Residual analysis module is conducted to analyze the performance of active power estimation and anomaly detection.

In the proposed system, LSTM-AE excels in capturing temporal dependencies and complex patterns in PV system data, enabling effective system dynamics modeling and anomaly identification through reconstruction of normal data patterns. Its ability to learn meaningful data representations reduces the need for manual feature engineering. In addition, LSTM-AE for anomaly detection in PV system faces challenges in terms of computational cost, requires substantial high-quality data, and is susceptible to overfitting and noise, while accurately defining anomalies remains complex. Daily irradiance variations significantly impact LSTM-AE anomaly detection for photovoltaic panels, offering potential benefits in feature richness and anomaly differentiation while increasing model complexity, risk of overfitting, and false positive rate.

The rest of this paper is organized as follow: Section 2 describes overview of solar power plant; Section 3 present the structure of the proposed method including data acquisition process and presentation of LSTM-AE. Section 4 illustrates the model development process including data pre-processing through exploratory data analysis, training dan testing LSTM-AE. Finally, this paper ends with Section 5 that consists of conclusions of this study and giving some perspectives issues which relates to solar power plant.

Similar research of PV yields prediction was conducted in [17] which worked on irradiance and temperature data forecasting for prediction the active power in PV system. Lee in [18] performed research of a solar power forecasting technique by utilizing convolutional neural networks (CNN) and long–short-term memory (LSTM) networks. They empirically confirmed that their method can predict the solar power well with roughly estimated weather data obtained from national weather centers. Another research related to power forecasting in PV system that employs sky images information and tailored ANN techniques for predicting the weather and solar irradiation was investigated in [19]. Highly detailed energy yield forecasts were produced in their research for time horizons up to 15 min and resolution of one second. Al-Dahidi et al. in [20] proposed an ensemble approach of an optimized ANN for solar photovoltaics power prediction for 24 h horizon. Their proposed system was applied to 231 kWac capacity located on the rooftop of building and was claimed successful for power prediction with high confidence level up to 80 %. Moreover, the use of deep learning thru CNN and LSTM in photovoltaics power forecasting was reported in [21]. Their works used CNN model to discover nonlinear features and invariant structures exhibited in the historian power data and applied LSTM to model the temporal changes in the latest data PV and to estimate the power for the next future.

The application of forecasting method using machine learning (ML) to support predictive maintenance has been reported in [22]. They presented a study of the state-of-the-art comparing the technique to forecast solar irradiance and ambient temperature and their relations with predictive maintenance models. This review included evaluation of the suitability of the model for PV system taking on their forecasting horizon, computational cost and improvement opportunities for future work in PV industry.

### 5. Result and discussion

5.1. Anomaly detection

Anomaly detection is performed by feeding new data into the trained LSTM-AE model, which calculates the reconstruction error for each input sequence. If this error exceeds a predefined threshold, an anomaly is flagged, triggering an alert indicating potential system deviations from normal operating conditions.

Observing residual as depicted in Fig. 18, the loss of RMSE can be derived for determining the threshold that used to anomaly detection. RMSE can represents the standard deviation of normal operation condition of PV system, therefore, the determination for anomaly detection can be performed on the basis of such standard deviation (σh).

PV system which operates in normal condition produces behavior of the plant with not much differ from the predicted parameters. This behavior can be reflected from residuals with low values. In case of

Regarding this thresholds, new data that surpass HLL however still lower than HUL indicates abnormalities behavior occur in the system, while new data that exceed HUL indicates strong anomaly has been laid on the system. Moreover, if the dataset is not featured a normal distribution, at least 88.9 % of the data points fall within 3σh. Based on this reason, the justification of HLL and HUL with values of 3σhand 5σh, respectively, are reasonable.

In addition, in order to increase the accuracy of recognition of abnormalities in data samples, minimizing the probability of false positive in warnings must be considered. It needs further processing in earlier step of anomaly detection. For example, how to create trend that does not content many peaks in residuals but have information of abnormalities. Many peaks are resulted from high frequency in dataset but this will tend to produce false positive in warnings. Therefore, filtering is needed in this step to extract and highlight the trend in residuals. Moving averaging using triangular function can be selected as filtering method and window size must be specified for computation in averaging process. Window size can be determined using autocorrelation function that highlights the periodicity in residuals. The followings are explanations of process for determining window size:

- ■ peaks finding is performed to the output of autocorrelation function and these points are corresponded to short-time period (Fig. 19);
- ■ short-time period is averaged based on related time values and shorttime period average (SPA) is calculated;
- ■ long-time period is determined by featuring all peaks of autocorrelation that have values greater than 0.2. The points, x-picked in Fig. 19, show the selection of time difference which is larger than SPA;
- ■ those selected time difference are then averaged to determine longtime period average (LPA) and the size for windowing is set to 2LPA.


The calculation of SPA and LPA from the dataset gives value of 24 h and 48 h, respectively. This means the window size of triangular moving averaging is set to 48 h. Moreover, the normalized residual trend is computed with triangular moving averaging is depicted in Fig. 20.

Fig. 21 shows the active AC power anomalies detected in inverters F1 and F2. The proposed method is proven to be able to recognize and capture anomalies generated in the inverters during operation. When the power drops to a very low value, the proposed method will flag and alert the operator to take necessary maintenance actions.

In summary, the description of how the proposed LSTM-AE method is used for anomaly detection is as follows: the LSTM-AE model is trained on historical data from a PV system, including sensor readings such as active power, solar irradiance, and ambient temperature. The model consists of two main components: an encoder that compresses the data and a decoder that reconstructs it. Through training on data that represents normal operation, LSTM-AE learns to accurately recreate typical system behavior. When the reconstructed data deviates significantly from the actual data, it signals a potential anomaly, indicating a possible fault in the system. To identify these anomalies, the residuals—the difference between the actual and predicted data—are assessed and thresholds (HLL and HUL) derived from the Loss RMSE distribution are applied to determine whether the anomaly is significant. - 5.2. Predictive alerts


Predictive alerts are created to give warnings to the operator of PV system that some anomalous are possible happened for the next days or hours before failure occurs. These warnings are beneficial to power plant manager who has responsibility to schedule maintenance activity addressed at avoiding failures and possible loss of power production.

Predictive alerts are created by finding the trend of degradation related to the inverters condition by means of calculating derivative of the residual trend. If the derivative of such trend falls into positive number means that the new samples relate to degradation phenomena in the inverter. The residual trend tends to approach the upper value

threshold and the system is suffering anomaly. While if the derivative of residual trend is negative values, then the system tends to normal condition.

### 6. Conclusions and future work

The aim of this study is to develop predictive maintenance model based on anomaly detection applied to PV system with minimal complexity of computation but beneficial in real application perspective. The resulting predictive maintenance model is capable of providing hourly predictive alerts to warn operators and support them in the maintenance decision-making process. The developed model is built using SCADA data obtained from the PV power generation system with one-minute interval acquisition and learning through ML method called LSTM-AE to estimate the inverter model. The results show that the anomaly prediction can achieve reasonable accuracy with minimum test errors MSE, RMSE, and MAE of 10.95, 3.30, and 2.76, respectively, and can be applied to the PV system.

The results suggest that accuracy of prediction and anomaly detection models still need to be improved in order to produce financial impacts for the solar power plant. This is a future work as a continuation of the research. Furthermore, the improvement of prediction seems to play a more crucial role for the financial performance of the solar power plant. We are investigating the viability of a hybrid model of ARIMA and

machine learning for predicting the power output of the solar power plant. ARIMA’s strength to capture the linear trend can be combined with the machine learning model for the residuals. In addition to the development of the hybrid models, data enrichment to include meteorological data from satellites seems to offer further opportunities for improving the accuracy of solar power plant output predictions.
